AI is changing what it means to be a strong individual contributor. Not by replacing craft, but by amplifying it — and the best designers are becoming one-person studios.

I call this the **Super IC**: someone who leverages AI tools to produce work that previously required a team. Not a generalist who does everything poorly. A specialist who uses leverage to do their specialty at a scale that wasn't possible before.

![The best designers are becoming one-person studios](/images/placeholder-ai.svg)

## What makes a Super IC

Three things:

1. **Deep craft knowledge.** You need to know what good looks like before you can direct AI to produce it. A senior designer with AI produces better work than a junior designer with the same tools.
2. **Taste as a skill.** When everyone can generate anything, the person who can tell the difference between good and great becomes the bottleneck.
3. **Systems thinking.** Super ICs don't just produce outputs. They build systems — prompts, workflows, and frameworks that make their leverage reproducible.

## The shift in org design

This has implications for how teams are structured:

- **Small, senior teams** outperform large, junior teams armed with the same tools.
- The **bottleneck moves** from production to direction. More people can make things. Fewer people can decide *what* to make.
- **Review and curation** become the core leadership skill, not delegation.

## The uncomfortable truth

Not everyone becomes a Super IC. It requires a combination of craft depth, taste, and technical comfort that's still rare. And it requires the discipline to use AI as leverage rather than a crutch.

The designers who thrive aren't the ones who generate the most. They're the ones who **edit the hardest**.

## What I'm watching

- How orgs rebalance between ICs and teams
- Whether AI-native designers develop fundamentally different workflows
- Whether "AI-assisted" becomes a meaningless qualifier (like "internet-assisted" did)
- Whether taste becomes a more explicit hiring criterion

> The age of the Super IC isn't about AI replacing designers. It's about designers who understand leverage outperforming those who don't.
